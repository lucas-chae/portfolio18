ENJOY - REDESIGN

Technology meets People.

Enjoy is a service that provides a new kind of delivery experience. It’s so much more than just bringing the product to your door. Users can connect and communicate with friendly to learn about the product they purchased. Just like the service Enjoy offers, its website provides a lot of warm and thoughtful details including profiles of the experts and videos of the customers interacting with those experts.

DESIGN CHALLENGE

Redisgn: A plan for making changes to the structure and functions of an artifact, building or system so as to better serve the purpose of the original design.

USER INTERVIEW
TL;DR

Conduct appropriate interviews to identify problems with existing design.

The purpose of the original Enjoy webstie is to introduce the service, and help the users use their service. Since it is already a design that has been implemented and being used, I thought the best way to approach the redesign process is through user interviews and heuristic analysis, for the existing website. Interview method: Speak out your thoughts: People I already know, speak their thoughts as they experience the user interface. Thoughts and actions can truly show the effectiveness of Interface design and whether the ui flow is working properly. I also asked them to purchase a product through enjoy.

Archetype
Average Joe

Intrigued by the video, but didn’t click “See how enjoy works” • Very amused by the infographics • Had no idea what product they sold, or affiliated with. • Didn’t like how it’s listed by companies, (For someone who’s not familiar with it, doesn’t make sense to list it by companies • When asked a question, they went to Sonos. Some questions were “Do I have to sign in?” and what not. • At the purchase page they were directed to, it simply said home delivery, not Sonos. Said he won’t use it because of the lack of direction.

Archetype
Tech-Novice

Also very intrigued by the video, but didn’t click on “see how enjoy works”. Said it looked pretty, but didn’t really tell how it worked. • Doubted about the 30 minute delivery • Never heard of the companies before, so did not use what products they sold.

Archetype
Tech-Guru

Got bored of the video midway, because he expected it to actually tell him how it worked. • Was curious as to why same-day delivery is needed, since many high-end tech-products are bought after many days of research and consideration. • Also confused about the company lsiting. • Liked the “personal” people • Didn’t read the description on the left when purchasing. Confused about the activation of Enjoy. • Wanted to know about the target audience and demographics
UI DESIGN HEURISTICS
Wireframe

Main Screen

1. Header

Header now has
• How it works
• Where we deliver
• Our experts
• Shop
The original design confused many users because too much information was presented in a long linear frame. By reducing the contents to three similar height elements, the progression through the scroll will be more intuitive.

2. Video

People loved the front-page video, but hardly ever clicked on "See how it works". unnecessary element should be deleted

3. How it works

The infographic was understood very well by all users. No change is needed.

4. Where we deliver

The card slide on the right column was never used by the users. Sliding through numerous delivery locations seemed inefficient. The information would be presented more effectively when users can see all the delivery locations in one block. Search block on the left column can remain the same.

5. Our experts &

6. Our customers

Users never swiped more than three expert/customer profiles, and many users found them unrelatable. In order to increase the chace of users seeing a relatable profile, three default profiles that represent distinct demographics could be displayed. Other expert/customer profiles can be shown at the buttom as thumbnails, with an option to view more.

Shop Page (Main)

One of the major issuess with the orignal design was the shopping interface Users with no previous knowledge on the brand could not figure out which items were available. Most of the users watned to see different products in one page, instead of brands. To make the whole shopping experience more intuitive, I added a seprate page dedicated to purchasing items.

7. Intro-shopping

A dedicated photo or video that represents the shopping experience can be displayed at the top of the page.

8. Brand/Product

Each card view will display what type of product the company sells, the logo of the brand, and 3 most popular items of the company.

Shop Page (Brand)

This is the page users will be directed to when they choose one of the brands from the main Shop Page.

9. Intro-brand

A photo that represents the brand can be displayed here.

10. Products

Brief information about products avaiable for purchase will be displayed.

11. Purchase instructions

The most confusing interface in the previous design was the actual purchasing. Becuase different brands had their own website and user interface, a lot of the users found it difficult to apply Enjoy service, or simply did not know if it had been activated. In fact, half of the users failed to activate Enjoy correctly. Displaying instructions on how to apply Enjoy service for that specific brand, before users get redirected to the purchase page, will greatly help the users to make the purchase properly.

11. Customer reviews

Same as #5 and #6.
